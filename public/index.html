Additionally, there are some tools to see more information about your music.

Song Info

Audio Features:

Spotify doesn't just categorize music by Genre; here are some other statistics it keeps track of.

Acousticness

A confidence measurement that the track is acoustic.

Danceability

Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm, stability, beat strength, and overall regularity.

Energy

Energy represents perceptions of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic songs are fast, loud, and noisey. Features that contriubute to this attribute include dynamic range, percieved loudness, timbre, onset rate, and general entropy.

Instrumentalness

A confidence measurement that the track is instrumental. A value over 50% are instrumental songs.

Liveness

A confidence measure that a audience is in the track. Values over 80% implies a strong likelihood the track is live.

Speechiness

Presence of spoken words. Values over 66% are tracks with mostly only spoken words. From 33% to 66%, there is a good chance the track contains both music and speech, either in sections or in layers, such as in rap music. Music below 33% are most likely music and non-speak-like tracks.

Valence

Musical postiveness. The higher the valence, the more positive (happy, cheerful, euphoric), while low valence sound more negative (sad, depressed, angry).

Tempo

Overall estimated tempo of a track in BPM. Tempo is the speed or pace of a given track, and is derived from the average beat duration.
